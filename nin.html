What is a NIN?

Key facts

  • The National Identity Number (NIN) is an 11-digit unique identifier issued by NIMC вЂ” the National Identity Management Commission of Nigeria.
  • It is your permanent, lifelong identity token as a Nigerian citizen. It does not expire and cannot be reassigned.
  • It links your biometric data (fingerprints, facial image) to your personal record in the Nigerian national identity database.
  • Eclat Synergy is an officially licensed NIMC enrolment partner via FUWA Agribusiness, authorised to capture diaspora NIN applications across the UK and Germany.

Whether you are enrolling for the first time, recovering a lost NIN, correcting an error, or registering a child, our trained staff guide you through every step вЂ” in full compliance with NIMC standards. You do not need to travel to Nigeria.

Step-by-Step Enrolment Process

A simple, transparent process вЂ” from pre-enrolment to NIN issuance.

  1. Complete your NIMC Pre-Enrolment online

    Visit penrol.nimc.gov.ng to fill your pre-enrolment form. All mandatory fields must be completed accurately. Print your pre-enrolment slip with its 2D barcode вЂ” you must bring this to your appointment. If you are unable to pre-enrol online, our team can assist you on the day.

  2. Book your appointment (at least 24 hours in advance)

    Call or WhatsApp +447450 444858 to reserve your slot at your nearest centre. Walk-ins are not guaranteed вЂ” booking ahead is strongly recommended. You can also book multiple family members in one visit.

  3. Document review & preparation

    Our team reviews your documents ahead of or on arrival to flag any issues that could cause a rejection. We check your pre-enrolment form, identity documents and supporting materials вЂ” so you submit with confidence, not guesswork.

  4. Visit your nearest centre with all originals

    Attend your appointment with your pre-enrolment slip, all original documents, and your payment. Our enrolment officer will verify your details, scan your documents, and confirm your data before biometric capture begins.

  5. Biometric capture (~15вЂ“30 minutes)

    Our officer captures your fingerprints, facial photograph and other biometric data using NIMC-approved equipment. Data is transmitted directly and securely to the NIMC national identity database. Your personal information is not stored locally after processing.

  6. Receive your Transaction ID Slip

    After successful capture, you will receive a Transaction ID Slip as confirmation. Keep this safe вЂ” you will need it to collect your NIN slip if it is not issued on the same day.

  7. NIN issued

    Your NIN slip may be issued on the day (subject to NIMC processing) or within a few working days. NIMC will also send your NIN via SMS to the mobile number registered during enrolment. If you opted for a NIN PVC card, delivery takes approximately 10 working days.
